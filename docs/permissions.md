# Who can do what — roles, permissions & review

A plain-language reference for **The Fin AI community**. If you're an officer and
unsure whether you can do something — or where your edit went — this is the page.

In **Phase 1** the people who log in are **officers**; most researchers exist as
**member cards** that an officer keeps up to date until the person signs up and
**claims** their card.

---

## The roles

| Role | Stewards | Lives on |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Chapter Officer** | the **people** of one chapter — their skills, levels, available time | People page + each member's profile |
| **Working Group Leader** | the **projects** of one working group | Projects page + each project |
| **First Author** | one **project** they lead (a seat, matched like any need) | that project |
| **President / Admin** | everything, across all chapters & groups | Admin (Settings) |

A person can hold more than one role.

---

## Chapter Officer — you manage your chapter's people

| You can… | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Add a person** | People → "Add a person" (name + email). Creates a card you manage. |
| **Edit any registered member in your chapter** | Their skills, skill level (Learning / Independent / Lead), and monthly **Available time** — applied **directly** (you're trusted). This includes claimed members, not just cards. |
| **Match people to needs** | From inside a project: pick an open need, see ranked candidates with spare capacity, Assign. The bar turns red before you over-commit anyone; if someone doesn't qualify the error names the missing skill + level. |
| **Remove / replace** | Each seated person has a **Remove** on the project team (frees the seat, reopens the need). |
| **Archive a card** | On a card's profile, "Archive this card" (for cards added by mistake — reversible by an admin). |
| **Review self-submitted changes** | When a member edits *their own* card, it comes to you — see **"Changes awaiting your review"** on their profile and **Approve / Reject**. |

You **cannot** edit people outside your chapter, or another chapter's officers (unless you're also an admin).

## Working Group Leader — you manage your group's projects

| You can… | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Create or claim a project** | Free, no bond. Its first-author seat starts open. |
| **Run the record** | Task board (add / edit / **delete** tasks, set owners & status), draft links, meetings. |
| **Post needs** | "Post a role" — a skill at a level, or a resource. Edit a need while it's unfilled; **remove** an unfilled need. |
| **Move status** | Status changes **ask to confirm**; Hold / Resume are one-click. **Finish** is irreversible and asks twice → goes to review, then settlement. |
| **Archive a project** | "Archive project" (for test/abandoned projects — reversible by an admin). |
| **Settle** | When finished, split the pool by logged contribution with a fairness check. |

## First Author — you lead one project

You're matched into the lead seat like any other need. On your project you have the
same record powers as the group leader (tasks, links, meetings, needs). At settlement
the pool is split by contribution — First Author is a role, not an automatic bigger share.

---

## Where your edits go (the review flow)

- **An officer editing a card/member they manage → applied immediately.** No queue.
- **A member editing their own card** (skills, level, hours) → **submitted for review**. The field shows a "pending review" chip, and the change goes to **their chapter officer** (or an admin), who sees it on the member's profile and clicks Approve / Reject.
- **A new resource** (compute, data, funding, extra people) → reviewed by a **steward** before it can be offered to projects.

**Who reviews whom:** a member's self-submitted change → an **officer of that member's chapter** (or an admin). There is **no fixed time limit** — it waits in the officer's "Changes awaiting your review" panel on the member's profile until they act. If something's urgent, ping the officer directly; the review is one click.

---

## Available time vs resources (so the numbers don't confuse)

- **Available time** = a person's *own* monthly hours (one number, on their profile). This is what gets committed when they're assigned to project work.
- **Resources** = things they can *additionally* offer — compute, data, funding, or **extra people** (e.g. RAs, students). Listed separately, reviewed by a steward.

These are two different things on purpose; a person's own time is **not** also listed as a resource.
